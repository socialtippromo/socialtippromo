How I got here

My brain is hyper-logical, and solving problems is soothing. That’s been true since I was a kid. If something’s broken, I want to understand the system and fix it—whether that’s wiring a campervan, learning how to plaster a wall, building an analogue synth, understanding a niche industry just to solve the niche problems, or figuring out a simple solution for my kid's complex questions.

I don’t chase shiny features; I chase working systems in the real world. My experience shows solving problems and building products is simple, if you get the right people, and the right framework, it succeeds. I’m an empath and a collaborator. I say “I don’t know” a lot, because I don't, but we figure it out together, and when I do say “I know how to solve this...” it’s earned and trusted.

About 5 years ago, I shifted from a Product Manager doing tickets for engineers, to a leader setting direction the moment I realised the bigger problem that needs solved is the strategy: choosing the lane, setting the doctrine, and building a framework the team can execute against.

How I work with others? I keep us honest by being honest myself. I share problems immediately, no gatekeeping of information or ideas, and we fix things together. I live in the detail when it matters, then translate it so design, engineering, and the business are aligned without jargon.

I was made redundant for non-strategic reasons. It's fine, I don't take it personally. It clarified what I want next: work with people I admire who are trying to change an industry, not decorate it.

Why Social Tip and why you? Because ads are dying and people trust people. We buy the car our mate says we should, we subscribe to YouTube channels based on our partners recommendations, and we listen to podcasts, because that colleague said it made them think differently. Trust in big, faceless machines is falling; trust is highest when the person recommending something has skin in the game. Social Tip can turn that truth into a product that compounds.

I’m not here to manage a backlog. I’m here to build a machine: verified recommendations that lead to conversions; conversions trigger fast, reliable payouts; payouts motivate repeat recommendations—and the loop compounds.
