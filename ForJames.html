How I got here

I’m autistic. My brain is hyper-logical, and solving problems is soothing. That’s been true since I was a kid. If something’s broken, I want to understand the system and fix it—whether that’s wiring a campervan, building an analogue synth, or sorting a simple problem for my kids.

I don’t chase shiny features; I chase working systems in the real world. My compass is simple. The framework defines whether a project succeeds; the people define whether the product succeeds. I’m an empath and a collaborator. I say “I don’t know” a lot because someone else might—and when I do say “this is the way,” it’s earned and trusted.

About 5 years ago, I shifted from “PM doing tickets” to “leader setting direction” the moment I realised the bigger problem is strategy: choosing the lane, setting the doctrine, and building a framework the team can execute against. Top-down solutioning isn’t about control; it’s about clarity—so everyone knows the game we’re playing and how we keep score.

How I run teams: I keep us honest by being honest myself. I share problems immediately, no gatekeeping of information or ideas, and we fix things together. I live in the detail when it matters, then translate it so design, engineering, and the business are aligned without jargon.

I was made redundant for non-strategic reasons. Fine. It clarified what I want next: work with people I admire who are trying to change an industry, not decorate it. The feedback I hear most from teams: I get into the detail and make it understandable for everyone.

Why Social Tip and why you? Because ads are dying and people trust people. We buy the car our mate loved. Trust in big, faceless machines is falling; trust is highest when the person recommending something has skin in the game. Social Tip can turn that truth into a product that compounds. And the BrewDog “punk” bit—do it differently, don’t wait for permission, ship fast, learn fast—that’s how I work.

I’m not here to manage a backlog. I’m here to build a machine: verified recommendations that lead to conversions; conversions trigger fast, reliable payouts; payouts motivate repeat recommendations—and the loop compounds.
